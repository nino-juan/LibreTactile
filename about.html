LibreTactile story


As part of my (Juan Nino) PhD research on equitable opensource hci tech development, we are developing an assistive technology to help visually impaired people navigate the internet. We weren't able to find simple, affordable and open source tactile interfaces for our project, so we decided to create our own and open source it to enable more people to develop tactile interfaces for assistive technology or any other field (gaming, XR, VR neuro-rehab, etc). Because such a task requires the resources and expertise from different fields, we decided to create LibreTactile to orchestrate these efforts.

We believe in the power, flexibility, innovation, and resilience that diversity brings into communities. So we invite people from different backgrounds, nationalities, and interests (including assistive technology, UX/rehabilitation research, consumer electronics, designers, arts, XR, and gaming) to benefit from and contribute to our mission.

Mission

To connect researchers, developers, users, and enthusiasts of tactile technology from different fields for the equitable research, development, distribution and support of open source tactile technologies for human-computer interaction.

Vision

Touching the digital.
